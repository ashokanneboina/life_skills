### Question 3 - Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit?
Celebrating after each successful completion of a habit is important because it increases our confidence and makes us more motivated to continue doing that habit. When we acknowledge even small progress, we feel a sense of achievement, which creates a positive emotion. This positive feeling encourages us to repeat the behavior again. Over time, repeated celebration strengthens our belief that we can maintain the habit, making it easier and more automatic.

### Question 6 - Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?
From the book's perspective  to become a habit easier the main factor is friction. If we reduce the friction that means less effort is needed for that habit so it becomes easier.

### Question 7 - Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?
From the book's perspective  to become a bad habit harder to do here also the main factor is friction. if we increase friction that means more effort is needed to perform that bad habit. So it is very likely we don't want to perform that habit.

### Question 8: Pick one habit that you would like to do more of? What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue obvious or the habit more attractive or easy and or response satisfying?
Habit I want to do more : Sleeping early
* To make the cue obvious - Set a fixed alarm at 10:00 PM as a reminder to sleep.
* To make it attractive - Creating a relaxing bedtime routine.
* To make it easy - Prepare everything in advance like finishing all works before 10:00 PM.
* To make it satisfying - Keep track of how many days i slept before 10:00 PM and appreciate my self for that.
-----------

### Question 9: Pick one habit that you would like to eliminate or do less of? What are the steps that you can take to make it make the cue invisible or the process unattractive or hard or the response unsatisfying?
Habit I want to do less : Using mobile before sleeping

* To make the cue invisible - Keep the phone far from me.
* To make it unattractive - Reminding that using mobile before sleep is not productive.
* To make it hard - Turn on restrictions on mobile after a specified time. 
* To make it unsatisfying - Note down how distracted and lazy the next morning after using phone.
