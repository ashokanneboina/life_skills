###  Question 1 – Strategies for Active Listening
- Give your full attention to the speaker.
- Stay on the topic being discussed.
- Avoid interrupting until the speaker finishes.
- Show interest using small verbal responses.
- Use positive body language like eye contact and nodding.
- Restate main points to ensure understanding.

###  Question 2 – Reflective Listening (Fisher’s Model)
- Listen carefully to what is being said.
- Be aware of the speaker’s emotions.
- Put the message into your own words.
- Ask follow-up questions for clarity.
- Note down important details.
- Confirm that everyone understands the same points.

###  Question 3 – Barriers to Effective Listening
- Background noise or mobile distractions.
- Cutting others off while they speak.
- Forming opinions too early.
- Losing concentration easily.
- Emotional stress or tension.
- Planning your reply instead of listening.

###  Question 4 – Ways to Improve Listening
- Focus completely on the speaker.
- Minimize distractions around you.
- Allow others to finish their thoughts.
- Ask simple and clear questions.
- Write notes when required.
- Repeat or summarize key ideas.

###  Question 5 – Situations Where Passive Communication Is Used
- To prevent arguments.
- When feeling shy or uncomfortable.
- While speaking to seniors or authority figures.
- When confidence is low.
- To avoid hurting someone.
- When feeling nervous.

###  Question 6 – Situations Where Aggressive Communication Is Used
- When feeling extremely angry.
- During stressful situations.
- When feeling ignored or disrespected.
- In serious disagreements.
- When patience is exhausted.
- After ongoing frustration.

###  Question 7 – Situations Where Passive-Aggressive Communication Is Used
- Feeling upset but not expressing it openly.
- When avoiding direct confrontation.
- Feeling hurt or overlooked.
- Difficulty sharing emotions clearly.
- Experiencing jealousy or annoyance.
- Suppressing frustration.

###  Question 8 – Steps to Become More Assertive
- Speak honestly and clearly.
- Use “I” statements instead of blaming.
- Respect your needs and others’.
- Say no in a polite way.
- Stay calm and confident.
- Address problems early.
